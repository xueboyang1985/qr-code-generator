Why Local QR Code Generation Matters

When you enter a URL or WiFi password into a server-based QR code generator, that data is transmitted to their servers. For sensitive information like WiFi passwords or business data, this is a privacy risk. Browser-based QR code generation using JavaScript libraries (like qrcode.js) means:

  • Your data never leaves your computer
  • No server logs of what QR codes you generated
  • Works offline after the first page load
  • No third-party access to your information

Frequently Asked Questions

Which QR code generator is truly free?
Our QR Code Generator gives you 10 free QR codes per session, no signup, no tracking, fully customizable.

Can I create QR codes for WiFi?
Yes — enter your SSID and password, and the QR code will let anyone connect with a scan.

Do QR codes expire?
Static QR codes (generated locally) never expire — they're embedded in the image permanently. Only "dynamic" QR codes (server-based) can expire.

Are my WiFi credentials safe?
With browser-based QR code generators, your WiFi credentials never leave your device. With server-based generators, they're transmitted to a remote server.
