What is a QR Code?

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can be scanned by smartphone cameras. They store information like URLs, text, contact details, or WiFi configuration — making them a bridge between the physical and digital world.

Types of QR Codes You Can Create

  • URL QR Codes: Direct users to a website or landing page. Most common use case.
  • WiFi QR Codes: Share WiFi credentials instantly. Scan and connect — no need to type passwords.
  • Email QR Codes: Pre-fill recipient, subject, and body for quick email composition.
  • Phone QR Codes: Trigger a call when scanned — perfect for business cards.
  • SMS QR Codes: Open messaging app with pre-filled number and text.
  • Plain Text QR Codes: Encode any text information for quick sharing.

Best Practices for QR Codes

  • Test before printing: Always scan your QR code with multiple devices before mass distribution.
  • Consider size: A QR code should be at least 2cm × 2cm for reliable scanning.
  • Use error correction: Medium (15%) error correction balances scannability with data capacity.
  • Contrast matters: Dark code on light background scans best. Avoid low-contrast color combinations.
  • Add a margin: Leave at least 4mm of quiet zone around the QR code.
